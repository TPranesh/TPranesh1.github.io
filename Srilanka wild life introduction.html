Gal Oye National Park



Gal Oya National Park, nestled in eastern Sri Lanka, is a haven of pristine wilderness and aquatic allure. The expansive Senanayake Samudraya reservoir sets the stage for boat safaris, revealing the mesmerizing world of Asian elephants, elusive sloth bears, and formidable crocodile species. The park's avian diversity adds to the allure, with waterbirds and raptors gracing the skies. Beyond its wildlife charm, Gal Oya seamlessly transitions from grassy plains to dense forests, offering diverse habitats for countless species. Boat safaris provide an intimate connection with the park's biodiversity, presenting an alternative perspective on nature's grandeur. With its unique blend of terrestrial and aquatic wonders, Gal Oya National Park invites exploration and promises a glimpse into the captivating beauty of Sri Lanka's less-explored gem.

Water Buffalo

To regulate body temperature and protect against parasites, water buffaloes frequently engage in mud baths. In Gal Oya, observing these animals wallowing in the mud along the reservoir shores is a common and fascinating sight.

Leopard Panthera pardus

In Gal Oya National Park, leopards showcase exceptional adaptability, navigating seamlessly through diverse landscapes, including grassy plains, dense forests, and around the Senanayake Samudraya reservoir. This versatility sets them apart, making each leopard encounter in Gal Oya a testament to their remarkable ability to thrive in various environments.

Knuckles Mountain Range



The Knuckles Mountain Range, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in central Sri Lanka, is a biodiversity hotspot boasting a rich tapestry of wildlife. This mountainous haven is home to diverse species, including the endemic purple-faced langur, Sambar deer, and wild boars. Trekking through the mist-covered peaks reveals a symphony of birdlife, with the Sri Lanka white-eye and spurfowl among the avian residents. The range's lush forests harbor a variety of reptiles and amphibians, contributing to the region's ecological significance. The Knuckles Mountain Range, with its scenic landscapes and abundant wildlife, offers a captivating adventure for nature enthusiasts seeking to explore the unique ecosystems of Sri Lanka.
